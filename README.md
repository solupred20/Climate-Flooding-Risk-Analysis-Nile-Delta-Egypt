# Flooding Risk Assessment in the Nile Delta (Egypt)

## Overview

This project presents a research-based analysis of climate change-induced flooding and environmental hazards in the Nile Delta, focusing on Al-Dahria Village (Dakahlia Governorate).

The study highlights how inland rural communities are increasingly vulnerable to indirect flooding due to sea level rise, land subsidence, and rising groundwater levels.

---

## Study Highlights

- Case study: Al-Dahria Village, Nile Delta
- Focus: Climate change impacts on inland flooding
- Approach: Literature synthesis, GIS insights, environmental analysis

---

## Key Findings

- Inland areas are vulnerable even without direct coastal exposure
- Sea level rise indirectly increases groundwater levels and waterlogging
- Land subsidence significantly increases flood risk
- Reduced sediment supply (due to High Aswan Dam) worsens soil degradation
- Chronic flooding affects agriculture and long-term sustainability

---

## Methodology

- Review of peer-reviewed studies
- Remote sensing & GIS-based insights
- Environmental and hydrological analysis
- Downscaling regional findings to local context

---

## Environmental Risks Identified

- Rising groundwater levels
- Soil degradation and salinization
- Waterlogging in agricultural land
- Increased flood frequency and severity

---

## Adaptation Recommendations

- Improve drainage systems
- Optimize irrigation practices
- Integrated water resource management
- Climate-aware rural planning strategies

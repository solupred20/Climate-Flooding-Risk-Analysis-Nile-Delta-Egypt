# Research Summary

This project analyzes flooding risks in the Nile Delta caused by climate change, focusing on Al-Dahria Village in Dakahlia, Egypt.

## Key Points

- Climate change is increasing flooding risks in low-lying delta regions
- Even inland areas are affected indirectly
- Main drivers:
  - Sea level rise
  - Land subsidence
  - Rising groundwater levels

## Impact

- Agricultural land degradation
- Waterlogging
- Reduced soil quality
- Long-term environmental stress

## Conclusion

Inland rural communities in the Nile Delta are increasingly vulnerable to climate-related flooding, requiring better adaptation strategies.
